# Category Theory Resources

## Knowledge

- [Book: _Categories for the Working Mathematician_ — Saunders Mac Lane (1971/1998)](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-1-4757-4721-8)
  The canonical reference, written by a co-founder of the subject. Terse, authoritative, assumes mathematical maturity. Use for: the definitive statement of a definition or theorem, the Yoneda lemma, limits/colimits, adjunctions.
- [Book: _Category Theory in Context_ — Emily Riehl (Dover, 2016) — free PDF](https://emilyriehl.github.io/files/context.pdf)
  Modern, beautifully written, free for personal use. Strong on motivation and worked examples across algebra/topology. Use for: a second, friendlier take on any core concept; especially good on Yoneda and naturality.
- [Book: _Basic Category Theory_ — Tom Leinster (CUP, 2014) — free on arXiv (1612.09375)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1612.09375)
  Short, gentle, built around the idea of universal properties. CC-licensed. Use for: first exposure to a topic, clean minimal definitions, the "universal property" lens the mission cares about.
- [Paper: "General Theory of Natural Equivalences" — Eilenberg & Mac Lane (Trans. AMS 58, 1945, pp. 231–294)](https://www.ams.org/journals/tran/1945-058-00/S0002-9947-1945-0013131-6/S0002-9947-1945-0013131-6.pdf)
  The founding paper of the field — defines category, functor, natural transformation. Use for: history, original motivation (the notion of "natural" isomorphism), primary-source grounding.
- [Wiki: nLab](https://ncatlab.org/nlab/show/category)
  Free, authoritative community wiki maintained by working category theorists. Pitched high (often higher-category-theoretic) and terse, so best as a cross-check and precise reference rather than first exposure. Use for: nailing down an exact definition, finding the general/abstract statement, following links between concepts. Cross-check its phrasing against Riehl/Leinster when learning something new.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [The n-Category Café](https://golem.ph.utexas.edu/category/)
  Long-running research blog by Baez, Leinster, and others. High signal. Use for: seeing how practitioners actually think and argue.
- [Category Theory Zulip / r/math, r/categorytheory](https://www.reddit.com/r/categorytheory/)
  Use for: asking questions, sanity-checking proof sketches, finding study partners. (User has expressed no preference for/against communities yet — revisit.)
- [Math Stack Exchange — category-theory tag](https://math.stackexchange.com/questions/tagged/category-theory)
  Use for: specific stuck points; many canonical questions already answered carefully.
