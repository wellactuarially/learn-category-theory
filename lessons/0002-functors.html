Category Theory · Lesson 2

Functors

The structure-preserving maps between categories.

A category packages objects and their arrows; a functor is the right notion of map between two such packages. It is what lets category theory compare one mathematical world with another and carry constructions across — and historically it is the reason categories were defined at all, as the domain and codomain of these maps.

histRecall from Lesson 1: Eilenberg & Mac Lane (1945) defined categories in order to define functors (and functors in order to define natural transformations). The motivating functors came from algebraic topology — homology and the fundamental group, both met below. Eilenberg–Mac Lane (1945).

1 · The definition

Let C and D be categories. A functor C⟶FD consists of two assignments,

defRiehl, Category Theory in Context, Def. 1.3.1; Leinster, Basic Category Theory, §1.2; Mac Lane, CWM, §I.3. Riehl PDF · Leinster · nLab: functor.
subject to two laws.

The data:

  • On objects — to each object A of C, an object F(A) of D.
  • On morphisms — to each arrow A⟶fB of C, an arrow F(A)⟶F(f)F(B) of D. (So F sends domain to domain and codomain to codomain.)

The laws (functoriality):

  1. Preserves composition. For composable A⟶fB, B⟶gC, F(fg)=F(f)F(g)
  2. Preserves identities. For each object A, F(idA)=idF(A)

A functor as just defined is covariant: it keeps the direction of every arrow. (A functor that systematically reverses arrows is contravariant — equivalently a covariant functor out of the opposite category; we take that up next lesson.)

co/contra"Covariant" is the default; "functor" unqualified means covariant. Contravariance and the opposite category Cop are treated in Riehl §1.2–1.3 and Leinster §1.2.

Convention reminder

In our left-to-right notation the composition law reads F(fg)=F(f)F(g) — order preserved. Most sources, composing right-to-left, write the same law as F(g∘f)=F(g)∘F(f). Reverse to translate.

The induced map on hom-sets

Packaging the morphism assignment by source and target: for each pair A,B, a functor gives a function

FA,B:C(A,B)⟶D(F(A),F(B)),f↦F(f)

These hom-set maps are where the future lives: when each is injective the functor is faithful, when surjective full — the vocabulary in which the Yoneda lemma is eventually stated. We only name them here.

fwdFull and faithful functors: Riehl §1.5; they are central to the Yoneda lemma (Riehl §2.2). Flagged now, developed later.

Why exactly these two laws

Functoriality is not bookkeeping for its own sake — it is precisely what forces a functor to transport structure without distortion. Because F respects composition and identities, it automatically carries isomorphisms to isomorphisms

lemma"Functors preserve isomorphisms": Riehl, Lemma 1.3.8. The one-line proof is exactly functoriality applied to ff−1=id. (It need not reflect them — Exercise 1.3.viii.)
— so any property phrased through arrows becomes an invariant that F ports from one category to another. That is the whole reason π1 can prove two spaces are genuinely different: a functor turns a hard question in one world into an easier one in another, and the answer survives the trip.

2 · Functors in the wild

The definition earns its keep through how much it already covers. Each example below names the object action and the morphism action — that pair is the whole content.

  • Forgetful functors. Grp⟶USet sends a group to its underlying set and a homomorphism to itself-as-a-function — it forgets structure. Likewise Top⟶Set, Vect⟶Set. Its partner, the free functor Set⟶FGrp (free group on a set), points ahead to adjunctions.
    exForgetful and free functors: Leinster §1.2 and Riehl §1.3. The free ⊣ forgetful pairing is the prototypical adjunction (Leinster Ch. 2).
  • The fundamental group. Top∗⟶π1Grp sends a pointed space to its fundamental group and a based continuous map to the induced homomorphism. Functoriality (π1(fg)=π1(f)π1(g)) is exactly what makes it a tool: homeomorphic spaces get isomorphic groups, so a group difference proves a topological difference. This is the kind of example category theory was invented for.
  • A homomorphism is a functor. Recall a monoid (or group) M is a one-object category BM: its arrows are the elements, composition is the operation, the identity is the unit. A functor BM⟶BN sends the one object to the one object and each element m to F(m) with F(mn)=F(m)F(n) — precisely a monoid homomorphism. The two functor laws collapse to the two homomorphism laws.
    deloopingThe one-object category of a monoid is its delooping BM; functors BM→BN = monoid homomorphisms. Riehl Ex. 1.3.x; nLab: delooping. (Functors BG→Set are exactly G-sets — a preview of representable functors.)
  • The hom-functor C(A,−). Fix an object A in a locally small C. Then C(A,−):C⟶Set sends an object X to the set C(A,X), and an arrow X⟶fY to the function "post-compose with f", h↦hf. This single family of functors is the engine of the Yoneda lemma — your stated destination — so it is worth recognizing now.
    yonedaRepresentable functors C(A,−) and the Yoneda lemma: Riehl §2; Leinster Ch. 4. We will build to this deliberately.

3 · Functors compose

Functors are themselves arrows, and they behave like it. Given C⟶FD and D⟶GE, the composite FG (do F, then G) is again a functor, and each category has an identity functor idC fixing every object and arrow. Composition of functors is associative with these as units — so small categories and functors themselves form a category, Cat.

catCat, the category of small categories and functors: Riehl §1.3, Leinster §1.2. Restricting to small categories keeps it a legitimate (large) category — the size bookkeeping from Lesson 1 at work.

A functor between preorders is exactly a monotone map: x ≤ y ⇒ F(x) ≤ F(y). Because a preorder has at most one arrow between any two objects, the composition and identity laws hold automatically. Source: Riehl, Exercise 1.3.ii.

Let 𝒞 be two disjoint arrows a → b and c → d; map into the poset 0 ≤ 1 ≤ 2, sending a → b to 0 → 1 and c → d to 1 → 2 (so F(b) = F(c) = 1). Then 0 → 1 and 1 → 2 lie in the image and compose in 𝒟 to 0 → 2 — which is the image of no arrow of 𝒞. The image is not closed under composition, so it is not a subcategory.
