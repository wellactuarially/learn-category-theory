Double oppositeestablished

(Cop)op=C. Reversing all arrows twice is the identity.

Lesson 3; Riehl §1.2.

Contravariant = covariant on the oppositeestablished

A contravariant functor C⟶D is the same data as a covariant functor Cop⟶D.

Lesson 3; Riehl §1.3.

Homomorphisms are functorsestablished

A monoid/group homomorphism M⟶N is exactly a functor BM⟶BN between their one-object (delooping) categories.

Lesson 2; Riehl §1.3.

Functors preserve isomorphismsto prove

If A⟶fB is an isomorphism in C and F is any functor, then F(f) is an isomorphism (and F(f−1)=F(f)−1). One line from functoriality.

Riehl, Lemma 1.3.8 (functors preserve isomorphisms); cf. Exercise 1.3.viii (they need not reflect them).

An inverse is uniqueto prove

A morphism has at most one two-sided inverse; hence "isomorphism" is unambiguous.

Riehl Exercise 1.1.i(ii).

Duality principleto prove / internalize

The dual of any theorem true of all categories is again true of all categories (apply it in Cop). One proof, two theorems.

Lesson 3; Riehl §1.2; Mac Lane §II.1.

The Yoneda Lemmatarget

For a locally small C, object A, and presheaf F:Cop⟶Set, natural transformations C(−,A)⇒F correspond naturally and bijectively to elements of F(A). The goal — see MISSION.
